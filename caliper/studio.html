Four operators.
A measured studio.

Caliper is small on purpose. We are four operators — research, build, voice, measurement — working on between two and four engagements at a time. We don't have a head of growth, a head of brand, or a head of pretty much anything. The work is the work.

This page documents the people, how we work together, what we charge and why, and the published refusals that define the studio more than its capabilities do.

THESIS

A marketing studio is the sum of its refusals more than its capabilities. Most agencies sell capabilities because capabilities are easy to list and refusals are expensive to choose. We don't think the world needs another agency with a capabilities deck.

We measure twelve specific variables in every diagnostic. Each of them gets to a single question that a capabilities deck cannot answer: does the team treat language as a contract, or as decoration? You can detect this in a marketing site in about three minutes once you know what to look for.

What follows is not a thesis about marketing in general. It is a thesis about how we operate as a small studio. If you read this page and decide we are wrong about how studios should work, please don't book a diagnostic — that's the cheapest disqualification we both get.

how we work three principles · the Friday session

Three principles.
One standing call a week.

The work is the work; the rest is process. We have three principles that shape every engagement and one weekly call (Friday, 90 minutes) that is the only standing meeting we hold. Everything else is written and asynchronous.

  1. 01

    Write before talking.

    We write to think. Every diagnostic, brief, and recommendation lands as a document, not a deck. We then talk about the document, not in advance of it. This makes calls shorter and decisions cleaner.

  2. 02

    One decision per call.

    The Friday session is for one decision, not five. We circulate the decision and the alternatives the day before, and the call settles it. If a call has to settle two decisions, we cut the second one and put it on next week's docket.

  3. 03

    Ship every fourteen days.

    An engagement that hasn't shipped anything in two weeks is a research engagement, not a build engagement. We can do either, but they have different pricing and different scopes. We don't blend them in the same contract.

What we charge,
and why.

The diagnostic is $9,500 fixed for fourteen days. We charge this because it is the smallest amount of money that still forces the client to take the engagement seriously and the smallest amount of time that lets us run all twelve measurements honestly. Lower would mean we'd cut something; higher would mean we're extracting rent on a thing the buyer can't yet judge.

The build engagement is $25–80K per project, three weeks maximum, one shipped artifact. The range is scoped from the diagnostic; we don't begin a build engagement without one. Within the range, the variable is mostly scope and time-zone overlap; we will say no to engagements where the time-zone overlap with our team is under three hours a day.

The retainer is $18,000 per month, three months minimum. Two shipped artifacts a month, one Friday session, all artifacts logged in your repository (not in a status doc). We hold a strict cap of four retainer clients at a time. When we hit the cap we say no and we mean it; we have turned away clients at this stage.

All three are post-paid: invoiced at the end of the engagement (Diagnostic, Build) or end of the month (Retainer). We do not require deposits and we do not accept retainer-as-deposit arrangements. If we ever can't ship, you don't pay.

FICTIONAL-STUDIO DISCLOSURE

Caliper is a fictional precision-marketing studio used as a portfolio piece in this repository. The studio does not exist as a registered entity; none of the four operators named on this page are real people; none of the case studies on the work page describe real engagements. The methodology, the pricing structure, and the operating principles are honest expressions of how a studio could plausibly operate; they are not statements about a studio that does.

The repository is also the second of two coexisting portfolio pieces. Halo, which lives at the repo root, is a deliberately opposite-pole study: a fictional editorial-dark growth-marketing practice with a gravity-field metaphor. Caliper is the mechanical-instrument opposite. The two were designed to be read in pair as deliberate contrasts.
