§ 02 / 09 — the spread test method / 1 of 12

What the agency
said, and what
shipped.

The first measurement in every diagnostic is the spread test: for each claim an agency makes on its site, what is the verifiable artifact in the world? "Strategic" — show the strategy doc. "Creative" — show the creative. "Growth" — show the growth.

Most spreads are wide. The four below are paraphrased from real category language. We measure the gap between the claim and the artifact in millimetres.

CLAIM 01
"Strategic creative."
→
Two decks; no strategy doc, no creative brief, no production calendar. Spread: 62.5 mm.
CLAIM 02
"Data-driven growth."
→
Two dashboards inherited from the previous agency; no measurement plan; no integration to the warehouse. Spread: 48.0 mm.
CLAIM 03
"Premium brand work."
→
A logo refresh and a Notion brand guideline. No system, no kit, no rollout. Spread: 55.0 mm.
CLAIM 04
"Full-funnel campaigns."
→
Top-of-funnel paid social; no MQL definition, no sales-handoff, no lifecycle. Spread: 71.0 mm.
§ 03 / 09 — the diagnostic method / 2–12 of 12

Twelve measurements,
two weeks, one brief.

The diagnostic is a two-week paid engagement. We don't pitch. We don't promise. We measure twelve specific variables across your current marketing system, write a 12–20 page brief, and walk you through it in a 90-minute playback. That's the whole engagement.

What follows is the full list, in the order we run them. No measurement is proprietary; we publish them because the value isn't the list, it's the calibration.

  1. 01
    Spread test
    For every public claim on the agency or marketing site, the verifiable artifact behind it. Measured in millimetres of distance between word and thing.
  2. 02
    Conversion-path resolution
    Decisions a visitor must make between the hero and the primary conversion. Fewer is usually but not always better.
  3. 03
    Proof density
    Number of named, role-attributed, dated proof artifacts per 1,000 words of body copy. Stock logos do not count.
  4. 04
    Money on the page
    Whether and where pricing appears. "Let's chat" is recorded as absent.
  5. 05
    Refusal surface
    Whether the team publicly states the engagements it will not take, and how specifically.
  6. 06
    Methodology depth
    Documented process steps visible to a non-customer, measured in pages and in actual decisions made per step.
  7. 07
    Brand-asset shipped count
    Quantity of brand artifacts (kit components, templates, design tokens) the previous agency produced and the current team can still locate.
  8. 08
    Sales-handoff continuity
    Distance from MQL trigger to first sales touchpoint. Measured in hours and in number of people the lead passes through.
  9. 09
    Lifecycle redundancy
    Number of touchpoints in the first 90 days after sign-up, weighted by which ones actually carry new information.
  10. 10
    Internal-tool spread
    Number of internal tools each marketer must operate to ship a week's work. Counted with their licence cost.
  11. 11
    Decision velocity
    Time from "we should change X" to "X is live for end users." We measure the slowest decision and the median, not the fastest.
  12. 12
    Attribution honesty
    How the team accounts for unattributable demand. Whether the answer is "we don't" or "we estimate it like this." We grade both.

§ 05 / 09 — refusals scope

Four things we
will not do.

A studio is the sum of its refusals more than its capabilities. The four below are the shapes of engagement we have decided are not for us. We surface them so you can self-disqualify quickly — there is no faster mismatch detector than a refusal list.

REFUSAL 01

Performance media operations

We don't run paid social, paid search, or programmatic. We will sometimes design the creative that media-buyers use, but we don't own ad accounts, set budgets, or report against lift. The work is real; it is just not ours.

REFUSAL 02

Personality-led founder content

We don't ghostwrite for founders. There are studios that do this well. We are not one of them, and we believe the work degrades under our voice more than it gains from our craft.

REFUSAL 03

B2C consumer brands

We don't take consumer brands. Our measurement system, pricing transparency, and the relationship between content and pipeline are all built around B2B SaaS and B2B services. The work translates poorly outside that range and we don't want to fake the translation.

REFUSAL 04

Pre-revenue startups

We don't take engagements before ~$500K ARR. There is nothing yet to measure. The right first marketing hire at that stage is someone in the room, not a studio on a retainer.
