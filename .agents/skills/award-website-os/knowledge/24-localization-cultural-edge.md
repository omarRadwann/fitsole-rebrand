# 24 — Localization and Cultural Edge

## Mission

Make websites feel real for their market, not globally generic.

## Localization Layers

### Language
- English
- Arabic
- bilingual
- local dialect where appropriate
- formal vs conversational tone

### Trust
Different markets trust different proof:
- phone/WhatsApp may outperform forms
- founder/team visibility may matter
- location and real photos may matter
- certifications/payment options may matter
- local reviews may matter

### Behavior
For Egypt/MENA and similar markets:
- WhatsApp CTA is often critical
- phone number should be visible for services
- Arabic RTL support may be needed
- payment/installment details can reduce friction
- Instagram/Facebook may matter more for some businesses
- map/location clarity matters for clinics/restaurants/services

### Design
Avoid blindly importing Western SaaS templates.
Consider:
- bilingual spacing
- Arabic typography
- RTL layout
- culturally appropriate imagery
- local proof
- local contact behavior

## Bilingual Site Tips

- Do not auto-translate brand slogans without rewriting.
- Arabic line-height needs care.
- Use Arabic fonts intentionally.
- Test RTL layouts.
- Icons with direction should flip.
- Numbers/currency/date formats should match audience.
- Avoid mixing English visual hierarchy with Arabic text without adjustment.

## CTA Localization

Examples:
- WhatsApp us
- احجز استشارة
- اتصل الآن
- اطلب عرض سعر
- احجز موعد
- شوف أعمالنا

## Smart Rule

A localized site with fewer effects can outperform a global-looking award site if it feels more trustworthy.
