# 01 — Business Research Master

## Mission

Turn a vague business type into a usable strategy brief.

## Research Areas

For every business type, determine:

### Category
- What does this business sell?
- Is it local, SaaS, product, service, e-commerce, portfolio, marketplace, or content?
- Is the user likely B2B, B2C, enterprise, luxury, mass-market, or niche?

### Buyer Psychology
- What does the buyer already know?
- What are they afraid of?
- What makes them trust?
- What makes them hesitate?
- What would make them take action now?

### Conversion Goal
Choose one primary goal:
- call
- WhatsApp
- book demo
- book appointment
- buy product
- request quote
- subscribe
- apply
- view portfolio
- contact

### Proof Needed
Rank proof:
1. Real outcomes
2. Case studies
3. Portfolio/examples
4. Before/after
5. Certifications
6. Process explanation
7. Team/founder credibility
8. Guarantees
9. FAQs
10. Honest demo/prototype

Never invent proof.

### Competitor Audit
Find 3–8 competitors or reference sites.

Extract:
- common copy patterns
- visual clichés
- missing opportunities
- trust signals
- content sections
- UX issues
- SEO language
- premium differentiation opportunity

## Cliché Library

### SaaS
Avoid:
- "AI-powered platform to streamline workflows"
- fake dashboards with empty metrics
- purple gradient blob hero
- three generic feature cards

Better:
- specific pain
- mechanism
- product proof
- workflow demo
- role-specific examples

### Dental/Clinic
Avoid:
- stock smiling people
- sterile blue-white templates
- generic "your smile matters"

Better:
- calm trust
- doctor credibility
- before/after if real
- appointment clarity
- pain/anxiety reduction
- insurance/payment clarity

### Construction
Avoid:
- random crane hero
- generic hard-hat stock photos
- "building the future"

Better:
- site control
- materials/process
- project proof
- timelines
- safety
- craftsmanship
- operational clarity

### Luxury
Avoid:
- overused black/gold
- fake exclusivity
- thin unreadable typography

Better:
- restraint
- material detail
- editorial layout
- confident negative space
- tactile imagery
