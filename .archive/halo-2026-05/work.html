⌜MMXXVI⌝ — Casework, 2014—2026

Twelve engagements, twelve durable shifts.

We don’t take on creative work. We take on operating mandates — typically six to thirty months — where the bet is positioning, content systems, and a slower, more deliberate operating cadence. What follows is a curated twelve of the eighty-three engagements we’ve run since 2014.

Shown 12 of 83 Range 2014 — 2026 Median engagement 18 months Categories 9
  1. Case 01 · Reykjavík · 2026

    Halcyon Aero — from chartered jets to a category of one.

    A young Icelandic private-aviation co-operative burning $42K a month on paid search against generic “private jet” terms. We retired the paid-search machine and replaced it with an editorial system: weekly long-form on flight ops, founder interviews on the ramp, a public tradecraft archive. The brand is now cited as a peer of operators ten times its size.

    Brand system Inbound 12-week build · 9-month partnership
    Qualified inbound
    +318% · 90 days
    Cost per booked charter
    $1,840 → $310
    2026 · Reykjavík, IS Brand · Inbound
  2. Case 02 · Copenhagen · 2025

    Soft Mass — a quiet skincare brand leaves paid acquisition behind.

    One ceramide-rich moisturiser. Spending 41% of revenue on Meta against a 2.1× MER and watching it fall every quarter as iOS attribution decayed. We took a polite knife to the funnel — paid halved on day one and rerouted into a weekly editorial column from the formulator, a referenced ingredient archive, and a podcast that refuses brand mentions.

    DTC Editorial system 14-week build · ongoing
    CAC
    −31% · 12 months
    AOV (refill subs)
    +47%
    2025 · Copenhagen, DK DTC · Editorial
  3. Case 03 · Berlin · 2025

    Ember OS — repositioned out of the AI productivity glut.

    A Series A AI-productivity tool. Beautiful product, indistinguishable category — one of forty companies promising “your AI co-pilot for work.” We didn’t change the product. We changed the sentence. Ember repositioned as “the calmest team OS” — a deliberate retreat from the AI arms race, leaning into focus and ritual.

    Demo bookings

    2025 · Berlin, DE Platform · Positioning
  4. Case 04 · Boston · 2024

    Tidewater — a 60-year fund finds a contrarian voice.

    A sixty-year-old value fund with a contrarian thesis they couldn’t articulate in marketing collateral. We pulled out the thesis in a hundred and twenty interview hours and put it in print. Quarterly long-form essays under each partner’s byline. A position-level transparency archive going back fifteen years.

    Finance Voice 16 weeks · advisory ongoing

    4.2× · 12 months

    2024 · Boston, US Finance · Voice
  5. Case 05 · Stockholm · 2024

    Ironwork — a Swedish foundry turns its catalogue into a magazine.

    Ninety years of bronze door pulls, drawer handles, and architectural details. Beautiful, brutalist, expensive — and sold almost entirely B2B through architects. We turned the catalogue into a magazine. Each product became a long-form essay on its making — the foundry shift it came off, the architect it was first specified for.

  6. Case 06 · Lisbon · 2023—2024

    Bloomwork — twelve microsites, one compounding audience.

    A slow-music label — twelve releases a year, mostly ambient and modern classical, distributed direct. No email list, no retail strategy, no idea who their audience was beyond a Bandcamp loyalty group. We built twelve microsites — one per release — and let them compound.

  7. Case 07 · Amsterdam · 2023

    Bay Mineral — DTC supplements after the subscription crash.

    A premium magnesium and electrolyte brand that built its first $4M ARR on Facebook prospecting and watched it evaporate when CPAs tripled in eighteen months. We pivoted to a content engine — a daily ingredient ledger, founder-led video on sleep science, and a referral programme designed for retention, not new-customer churn.

  8. Case 08 · Zürich · 2023

    Atelier Forge — an architecture firm rewrites its voice.

    A 22-partner practice whose website read like every other firm’s — competent, expensive, indistinguishable. Their work didn’t deserve the silence. We rebuilt the voice around the partners’ actual arguments: about scale, about restraint, about which clients to refuse. RFPs began arriving with the firm named explicitly.

  9. Case 09 · Toronto · 2024

    Praxis Health — from paid leads to PR-driven pipeline.

    A clinical-decision-support platform built for primary care. Marketing was running on cold lead-gen with a 60-day cycle and a fragile CMS that couldn’t carry weight. We rebuilt the brand around published research, hospital case studies, and a clinical advisory board that wrote on the record under bylines.

    Sea Beam — a yacht charter brand learns to charge for taste.

    A mid-market Mediterranean charter operator competing on price in a category where price is the wrong axis. We rebuilt the brand around the captains — twelve of them, each with a public profile, a written philosophy of charter, and a publicised refusal list. Booking value followed.

    Northern Index — an asset manager finds its first inbound quarter.

    A €4B asset manager whose entire LP funnel ran on conferences, warm intros, and golf. We didn’t replace any of that. We added a quarterly letter the partners actually wanted to write — a market memo with strong opinions and named bets. Inbound LP enquiry became a real channel for the first time in firm history.

  12. Case 12 · Edinburgh · 2022

    Tilden & Co. — an estate-planning firm shifts from referrals to organic.

    A 30-year private-client practice whose entire pipeline was warm referrals from accountants and family offices. Demand for their niche was rising faster than the referral network could carry. We built a long-form library — case-anonymised, deeply specific, and written by the principals — that ranked for the questions wealthy families actually search.
