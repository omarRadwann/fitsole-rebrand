Note from the practice · June ’26

The funnel was always a metaphor.

We borrowed it from physics, then drew it badly. A real funnel narrows what passes through to a specific outlet. A marketing funnel was supposed to describe how strangers became customers — but somewhere in the last decade it became the entire vocabulary, then the entire operating manual, then the entire strategy.

The result is the marketing many of you live inside: bid prices that never settle, creative testing that never compounds, attribution that doesn’t agree with revenue. The funnel narrative implies the work is to widen the top — to find more strangers — but the brands we know best win on the other end of the sentence: by being the kind of presence people end up at without having to be sent.

We use the word field for that. It is not a clever name. It is what physics calls a region of space within which an object exerts force on the things inside it. Brands are like that: gravitational. The work, then, is not to chase but to be — and the practice we run is the slow construction of that mass. We are willing to be wrong about this in public. We are very rarely wrong in private.

— Halo · Lisbon, June ’26
Engagement · Five phases

The five phases of a durable shift.

Every Halo engagement follows the same arc — field study, positioning, system, compounding loops, and the quarterly recalibration that separates a campaign from a category move.

01

Field study

Two weeks. Audience research, channel teardown, competitor and category audit, a written hypothesis signed by both sides. Nothing made yet — we settle on the bet first.

Weeks · 1 — 2
02

Positioning & voice

The one sentence the whole brand operates from. Three to four narrative directions pressure-tested in real placements before we ship a single line of copy externally.

Weeks · 3 — 4
03

System design

Brand identity, content pillars, channel mix, measurement plan. We build the editorial system before any media buys — owned channels live before the first dollar of paid.

Weeks · 5 — 7
04

Compounding loops

Ship, measure, iterate. Paid, owned, and earned move in one cadence with a weekly truth meeting. The dashboards are the only opinion that matters.

Months · 2 — 12
05

Quarterly recalibration

Every 90 days the brief is interrogated against the data. We cut what isn’t working, double the loops that are, and re-write the next quarter’s bet in plain language.

Every · 90 days
