## Visual Idea

The visual system should feel like a premium sneaker retail floor under a sharp verification layer: product wall, price tag, receipt, size desk, and drop board. It is energetic but not chaotic, premium but not fake luxury, and exact enough to earn trust.

## Palette

Core:
- Fitsole Red: primary accent for verification, CTAs, sale/active states.
- Warm White: main background.
- Graphite: primary text.
- Concrete: section background and dividers.
- Asphalt: dark contrast areas.
- Silver Rule: hairlines, borders, metadata.

Use color for function:
- Red: action, active, sale, verified, selected.
- Graphite: text and strong UI.
- Concrete/silver: structure.
- Green/blue/yellow only if needed for real system states and contrast-tested.

Avoid:
- Black/gold luxury cues.
- Purple-blue tech gradients.
- Random glow/orb backgrounds.
- One-note red overload.

## Material Direction

Use visual cues from:
- Rubber soles.
- Shoebox board.
- Matte labels.
- Receipt paper.
- Concrete retail floors.
- Mesh, leather, suede, knit, and stitching details if product media supports them.

Do not fake global brand product renders or use unauthorized campaign imagery.

## Typography Direction

Display:
- Condensed grotesk or sharp retail grotesk for drop/category headlines.
- Tight, confident, line-broken by meaning.
- No unreadable ultra-thin weights.

Body/UI:
- Neutral sans for product names, descriptions, filters, policies, checkout-adjacent copy.
- Highly legible at mobile sizes.

Metadata:
- Compact mono or tabular numeric styling for prices, sizes, SKUs, delivery windows, sale labels.
- Use sparingly.

Rules:
- Maximum two type families plus optional numeric/mono treatment.
- No viewport-width font scaling.
- Letter spacing should generally stay at 0 unless a specific all-caps label needs small positive tracking.
- Mobile type sizes must be manually composed.

## Layout System

Desktop:
- Strong product grid, editorial rhythm, occasional asymmetry.
- Product content starts early.
- Section bands are full-width, not cards inside cards.
- One hero, not many fake heroes.

Mobile:
- Product route within first viewport.
- Shorter headlines.
- Dense but readable 2-up product grids.
- Sticky search/cart and product CTAs.
- Category chips for faster discovery.

Spacing:
- Use consistent section spacing.
- Keep product cards compact but breathable.
- Dividers should feel like retail rules/tags, not decorative borders.

## Product Imagery

Product cards:
- Consistent aspect ratios.
- Clean product crops.
- Secondary image for hover/tap if available.
- No blurred, dark, or overly cropped product images that reduce inspection.

Hero/category:
- Real product or lifestyle image with direct shopping route.
- Mobile-specific crop required.
- Product should be recognizable in the first viewport.

PDP:
- Gallery starts with clear product image.
- Detail/macros if available.
- Lifestyle image only if it helps desire or fit context.

## Icon Direction

- Use simple line icons only where they clarify: delivery, exchange, payment, support, authenticity.
- Icons must be paired with text for trust claims.
- Avoid generic sparkles, rockets, shields without exact claim language.
- Confirm icon library license or draw a small owned set.

## UI Shape And Texture

- Buttons: crisp rectangles or subtly softened corners, 4px to 8px max unless existing system demands otherwise.
- Cards: product cards may use minimal framing; no nested card stacks.
- Borders: fine silver/graphite rules.
- Shadows: minimal; use real hierarchy, not glow.
- CTAs: high-contrast, stable, never hidden.

## Accessibility Rules

- Contrast-test all red/white/graphite combinations.
- Sale state must include text, not color alone.
- Focus ring should be visible and aligned with the red verification motif.
- Decorative textures should not reduce text contrast.
- Product information must not be image-only.

## SEO Rules

- Use real headings, not text baked into hero images.
- Preserve product and collection names in DOM.
- Collection intro copy should be concise and keyword-specific.
- Image alt text should identify product/category where meaningful.
- Decorative red-line graphics use empty alt or CSS.

## Visual QA Checklist

- Does the homepage look like Fitsole in five seconds?
- Are product, price, and shopping routes visible before the artistic system?
- Does the red verification motif feel owned, not decorative?
- Does mobile feel designed rather than compressed?
- Do product cards remain clear with long names and sale prices?
- Does the page avoid fake luxury, generic gradients, and hidden CTAs?
